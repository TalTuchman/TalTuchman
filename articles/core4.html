Forth Principle of Stratagema | Learning Patterns

Commanders or Leaders operating in a systemic environment that mediates between strategy and tactics, create both patterns of understanding and patterns of action. Once an operation is launched, they are in a constant search for alignment between the two.

Systems are the mechanism that mediates the tension between strategy (contextual understanding) and tactics (generic action). Herein lies the paradox: the more I know about the world, the more I realize I do not know.

The knowledge of knowledge compels. It compels us to adopt an attitude of permanent vigilance against the temptation of certainty. It compels us to recognize that certainty is not the proof of truth. It compels us to recognize that the world everyone thinks he sees is not the world but a world, which we bring forth with others

—Humberto Maturana & Francisco Varela

All strategists want to be Odysseus, the architect of history's first documented special operation, yet often find themselves identifying more with Sisyphus. The pursuit of strategic understanding and operational effectiveness never ceases. We must find a way to transform our institutions into constant learning machines that produce both.
