First Principle of Stratagema | Flow

In the spirit of Shakespeare's "the world is my oyster,": "the world is my teacher". It doesn't threaten; it instructs. It teaches through two primary dimensions: Flow, which is the logical order of things, and Variance, which is the potential for everything to change.

The world in itself is neutral—it is my own consciousness that defines it and gives it meaning. For a strategist, this interplay of Flow and Variance creates a constant opportunity to reinterpret the current order of things and decide how to operate within that order, or how to reshape it.

Therefore, strategy is inherently ecological; it is always changing and dependent on context. At its core, strategy is the potential to create change, which unfolds across four key dimensions:

  1. The capacity to change my own way of thinking.
  2. The ability to change my understanding of the world.
  3. The power to change my organization.
  4. And only after achieving the first three, the capability to change the world by intervening in a way that achieves my goals.
