Stratagema

Stratagema is different from corporate strategy by aiming to look at the world as a multi dimensional arena in which different stakeholders operate simultaneously. Stratagema is the process of researching the different elements and constraints the strategus faces when asking to accomplish a goal.

In its core strategy is really being honest and clear about reality. About your possibilities and constraints. If you understand that and are honest about that you’ll be able to analyze what’s going on and therefor build an effective strategy

Strategy stops people getting excited and wanting to do without fully thinking about what’s the most effective and why.

Strategy serves as the path leading from the current state to defined goals. Consider it akin to a map: mark your current location, the destination, and meticulously chart your course. The route may present cliffs, rivers, or obstacles necessitating circumvention. Walking a direct route without consulting the map might lead to falls or wasteful efforts overcoming avoidable obstacles.
