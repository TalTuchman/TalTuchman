Third Principle of Stratagema | Creating Freedom

Here we find another use for 'design'—the freedom to change the conditions and/or the theory of learning. This concept borrows from the Brazilian diplomat and philosopher José Merquior, who argued that theory should be a tool for action.

“Theory is not a pair of glasses, but a pair of guns. It does not allow one to see better but to fight better.”

Degrees of freedom thus become the quality benchmarks for any strategic inquiry and its inherent potential. The more consciously we define these freedoms, the easier it becomes to break free from the constraints of an existing paradigm and generate new understanding.
