Stratagema: The Art of Strategic Thinking

A Stratagema is not merely a plan, but a clever, often unconventional, maneuver designed to outwit an opponent or solve a complex problem. This philosophy, honed in the elite echelons of the military and the demanding boardrooms of global consulting, is my guide.

This ancient wisdom is more relevant today than ever. We win not through confrontation, but by preemptively building partnerships, demonstrating value, and leading with empathy. This is the modern stratagema: a blend of analytical rigor, human-centric leadership, and a relentless drive for clarity.

The Core Principles of Stratagema

Flow

The world is a teacher. Understanding its logical order (Flow) and its potential for change (Variance) is the key to strategy.

Explain

Strategy is an environment for thinking, not action. It is a theory of how the world works, tested through tactical systems.

Create Freedom

Theory is a weapon. Degrees of freedom become the benchmark for breaking paradigms and generating new understanding.

Learning Patterns

The paradox of strategy: the more you know, the more you realize you don't know. We must become constant learning machines.
